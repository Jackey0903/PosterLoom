01 · Overview

A poster is not a shrunken paper.

Turning a scientific paper into a conference poster requires compressing a long, multimodal document into one readable canvas. Existing systems often write content before the final geometry is known, then shrink, truncate, or drop material when it does not fit.

PosterLoom inverts that order. A structural template declares the capacity and visual policy of every region before content is generated. Specialized agents then select evidence, write to the available area, compose visuals, and render an editable PowerPoint. Deterministic checks and VLM review route only failed aspects to a bounded repair loop.

A

Print-ready by construction

Capacity-aware writing and native PPTX rendering prevent overflow, overlap, and unreadably small text.

B

Controlled one-to-many output

Template, style, density, background, and seed controls produce meaningful alternatives instead of accidental variation.

C

Validity-centered evaluation

A 621-paper benchmark reports defect-free Pass Rate alongside content, aesthetics, readability, and cost.

02 · Method

Structure first. Reflection after rendering.

Paper understanding, template-conditioned writing, reusable skills, native rendering, and bounded repair operate as one auditable pipeline.

1

Parse and ground

MinerU extracts text, figures, tables, and document structure while provenance remains attached to every asset.

2

Declare capacity

Template slot contracts expose geometry, reading order, prominence, character budgets, and compatible visual types.

3

Compose to fit

Content, template, layout, and visual agents select keypoints and place evidence against known spatial constraints.

4

Review and repair

Deterministic gates and VLM critics inspect grounding, overflow, readability, visual quality, and occupancy before acceptance.

03 · Controlled diversity

One paper, many valid posters.

Layout topology, typography, color, density, and background are independent controls. Diversity is created without sacrificing content fidelity or editability.

Cross-domain case study

Medical imaging, binary analysis, virtual dressing, and fairness benchmarking.

04 · Template library

Templates declare capacity, not content.

PosterLoom separates structural topology from visual presentation. Each slot specifies its geometry, reading order, semantic role, character range, bullet budget, minimum visual footprint, and figure/table compatibility before writing begins.

05 · Benchmark and evaluation

Validity is an outcome, not an assumption.

The benchmark spans five source partitions and ten research domains. PosterLoom evaluates whether a poster is usable before averaging its aesthetic or content score.

Pass Rate

Defect-free validity: no overlap, overflow, out-of-bounds content, missing assets, or unreadable core material.

Content fidelity

Paper-grounded keypoints, evidence coverage, and figure-text consistency.

Visual quality

Readability, layout efficiency, hierarchy, balance, and human preference.

Cost per valid poster

Generation cost is normalized by successful, usable output rather than raw attempts.
